SF2G is a cycling club

Commuting on 101 every day is the worst thing ever. 60-90 minutes in a car or corporate bus, each way, is dying a slow death. But there is another way.

Since 2005, SF2G has been riding bikes from SF to the south bay in a way that's reasonably fast, safe, and fun, with rides happening every workday in almost all weather conditions. Everyone is welcome, beginner to expert, from SF to any workplace down the peninsula.Try it -- it's addictive!

Getting there

    For simplicity, we'll assume SF to Mountain View.
  • Bayway The Usual : ~40 Miles.
    Bayway is a finely tuned (and somewhat convoluted) route honed over thousands of rides. It comprises a mix of roads, protected bike paths, and some downright weird stuff. Completely flat once you're south of SF, it's at times very beautiful and provides a view of the peninsula you'll not see any other way. This is a good ride for all skill/fitness levels.
  • Skyline Beautiful and Challenging : ~45 Miles.
    Skyline is a hilly and gorgeous route, and will improve your fitness quickly. Mostly comprised of CA-35, it's pretty much a straight line from the extreme west of SF all the way to the south bay. Recommended for more experienced cyclists.
  • Half Moon Bay Difficult and Awesome : ~62 Miles.
    Cruise down highway 1, go up the spectacular and legburning Tunitas Creek Road, and back down Kings Mountain Road's tight switchbacks. A truly stunning route that will burn a day's worth of calories. Recommended for fit & experienced cyclists.
